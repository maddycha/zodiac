THE GREAT RACE

The Chinese Zodiac is a cycle of 12 years, where each year is represented by one of twelve animals. There exists a story, in Chinese mythology, of a great race that decided which animals made it into the Zodiac and in what order.

The Jade Emperor decreed that the years on the calendar would be named for each animal in the order they reached him. To get there, the animals would have to cross a river.

RAT

Being unable to swim, the rat hatched a plan to ride on the ox's back across the river. Just as they were reaching the finish line, the rat leapt off to secure first place.

OX

Being so mild-tempered, the ox did not mind, and settled for second place.

While also riding on the ox's back, the cat was pushed into the river by the rat, and never made it into the zodiac.

CAT

TIGER

Although strong, the tiger was being pushed down the river by the current and ended up in third place.

RABBIT

The rabbit jumped between stones to cross the river and was blown by a gust of wind to the finish-line.

RABBIT

DRAGON

Although able to fly, the dragon ended up fifth as it stopped to bring rain to a nearby village. The dragon was also to thank for the wind that blew the rabbit to safety.

SNAKE

Hitching a ride in one of the horse's hooves, the snake was able to cross the river and take sixth place.

HORSE

Alarmed at the snake's sudden appearance, the horse delayed behind and ended in seventh.

SHEEP

Coming across a raft, the sheep banded together with the monkey and rooster to get across.

MONKEY

Using lengthy branches as makeshift paddles, the three furiously paddled to the river's shore.

ROOSTER

The rooster and monkey agreed to let the sheep take eighth for being so comforting and kind, followed by the monkey and rooster.

DOG

Despite being an adept swimmer, the dog came in eleventh place. It had not bathed in quite some time, and had over indulged in the water.

PIG

Lastly was the pig, who had stopped for a feast during the race. All of that food caused the pig to be sleepy, and it ended up taking a nap before making it to the finish line.
